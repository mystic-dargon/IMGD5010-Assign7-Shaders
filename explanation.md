# Inspiration of ERGOFLUX

Entering this assignment, I knew I wanted to experiment with expanding upon the sinewave function we looked at in class; in particular, I wanted to figure out how you could go about rendering multiple sinewaves of different colors, each capable of moving independently of one another. The resulting effect reminded me of the audio visualizers often used in techno music videos, which inspired me to try making something similar using a combination of pre-programmed responsiveness to audio levels and live-coded modifications to accentuate particularly powerful moments or key transitions in the audio track.

I was immediately drawn to using ERGOFLUX– a track created by my lovely partners– as the subject of my visualization for a couple of reasons: The energy level varies over the course of the song, which I felt I could capitalize on to create a compelling, evolving visualization that ebbed and flowed in intensity alongside the music, and the instrumentation of the track gave it this ‘buzzy’ quality that I felt would be well represented by the fluctuations of the sinewave(s). The final piece makes use of 4 main sinewaves: a blue one, set to respond to the lower frequencies; a green one, set to the middle frequencies; a red one, set to the higher frequencies; and a white one, set to the highest frequency band and used as a unifying point of stability (due to its generally lower magnitude than the other frequency bands). These sinewaves are then combined, multiplied, split, and modified over the course of the song to complement the music even further.
